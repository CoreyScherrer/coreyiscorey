How to take it to 11

A short guide to the visualizer

The visualizer reacts to whatever audio you give it. Pick a shape, give it a source, and the rest is dialing. This page covers the two things you need to start: what the shapes are and how to feed them sound. Everything else lives in the menu on the visualizer itself — open it with the ☰ button in the corner.

Section 1

The shapes

The Shape picker — middle row of the Soundboard, top of the menu — chooses what kind of object you're looking at. Each one reacts to the same audio signal differently. Cycle them; one will feel right for the track you're playing.

Bulb
The default Mandelbulb. Round, lumpy, slow. Reads as a living organism that breathes on the bass. Good for ambient, dub, anything slow.
Sacred
Geometric lattice. Hard angular surfaces — feels architectural and ritual. Treble cuts ridges into the surface. Pair with anything with crisp high-end.
Lava
Round molten blob. Smooth, balloon-like, bass-driven swelling. Reads warm and physical. Good for groove and bass-forward music.
Ferro
Ferrofluid — spikes on transients. Black chrome blob with sharp spikes that grow on bass hits. Continuous color rings drift across the surface. Built for percussive music.
Gasm
Colorgasm — psychedelic tunnel. No shape, just a descent into rings of color around a persistent bright center. Bass speeds the descent, mids twist the rings, treble shimmers the edges.
Section 2

Audio source

The Source section is at the top of the menu. You have three options for feeding the visualizer sound. They all work — the right pick depends on where the audio is coming from.

Tab
Best for streaming services. Click Tab, then choose the browser tab playing Spotify / YouTube / SoundCloud. Tick "Share audio" in the picker. The visualizer hears exactly what that tab plays — no microphone noise, no need to crank the speakers.
Mic
Best for live sound in the room. Click Mic, accept the permission prompt. The visualizer reacts to whatever your microphone picks up — a live band, a vinyl player, music on a speaker, you singing. Background noise will show up too.
File
Best for one specific track. Click File, drop an MP3 / WAV / audio file. The visualizer plays it back and reacts. Useful for testing how something looks against a specific song without sharing a tab.

If audio isn't reaching the visualizer, the first thing to check is the source — Tab needs "Share audio" ticked at the moment you pick the tab, and Mic needs the browser's microphone permission granted (the padlock icon next to the URL shows you which permissions are in play).

That's it

Pick a shape, share some sound

Everything else — sliders, presets, palette, quality — is in the menu on the visualizer itself. Open it, change things, see what happens.
