Digital scans are reference material — not evidence

Everything being uploaded to the family Drive folder is for finding and ordering the real thing. MUP requires originals or officially certified copies; plain scans and photocopies are rejected outright.

Common misunderstanding

A scanned birth certificate cannot be apostilled. Apostilles are only applied to originals or government-issued certified copies — so every vital record must be re-ordered as a physical certified copy from the issuing office, no matter how good the scan is.

Source: Croatian Citizenship Document Authentication and Submission Standards

When originals no longer exist

For the 1800s generations, if Croatian civil registers were lost or destroyed, MUP may accept alternatives — but they must still be physical certified copies ordered from the holding archive.

  • Church and baptismal records
  • Old Croatian identity documents (domovnica), military records
  • Ship passenger manifests · foreign naturalization papers

The order of operations is not negotiable

Each step must finish before the next begins. Doing them out of sequence means paying twice.

#StepWhy the order matters
1Order certified copyOnly originals/certified copies can be apostilled
2ApostilleState-level for vital records; federal for FBI check
3TranslateThe apostille stamp itself must also be translated
Costly mistake

Translating before apostilling means paying a second time to translate the apostille. Always apostille first, then hand the complete stamped document to the translator.

FBI background check — the tightest timing trap in the process

Valid 6 months from issue. The federal apostille that must follow it can take weeks to months. And you cannot file without an appointment, which may be a year out.

  • Must be the federal FBI Identity History Summary — state, county and local police checks are routinely rejected
  • Federal apostille comes only from the US Dept. of State Authentications Office, Washington DC
  • Every adult applicant needs their own, including spouses
  • Fingerprints can be submitted at a participating US post office
Sequence this backwards from your appointment

Book the consulate appointment first. Then count backwards: allow the apostille turnaround, and order the FBI check so the finished, apostilled, translated document is under 6 months old on the day you file. Order it too early and it expires in your folder.

"One and the Same Person" affidavit — this family will need one

MUP requires absolute legal harmony of names across every document. Where an ancestor appears as Vragnizan on one record and Vranizan on another, a sworn affidavit formally declares them the same person.

The affidavit is itself a legal document and follows the full chain: notarized → state apostille → certified translation.

If variations are extreme

A simple affidavit may not suffice, and a US court declaratory judgment ("One and the Same" order) may be required to amend the historical record. This can be procedurally difficult depending on state law — worth a lawyer's opinion early rather than discovering it late.

Source: Resolving Name Discrepancies for Croatian Citizenship Applications

CV (životopis) and motivational letter

Both must be in Croatian, or professionally translated with the English enclosed. Every adult applicant writes their own — including spouses.

The CV must explicitly state full name, date and place of birth, current address, education and career, parents' names and birth details, and the emigration path: who the Croatian ancestor is, exactly how you are related, and when and why they left.

The motivational letter covers your reason for applying, how the family maintained its Croatian connection, and your intent to contribute as a dual citizen.

Advantage worth using. Because this lineage reaches back generations, evidence of active cultural connection materially strengthens the claim to belonging to the Croatian nation. This family has exactly that: the Croatian Fraternal Union lodge and St. Mary Star of the Sea parish in Astoria. Name them.

Do not sign at home

Application forms must be signed in person, in front of the consular official, at your appointment. A form signed in advance will be rejected.

Dual citizenship is retained — the US does not require renunciation, and Croatia permits it under Article 11.

1. Military service — RESOLVED 23 July 2026

Both earlier versions were half right, and the answer changed in 2026. Croatia suspended conscription in 2008 — but the Sabor voted on 24 October 2025 to reinstate it, effective January 2026. That is why the older family notes disagree with the newer guidance.

What this means for a male dual citizen living in the US:

  • Registration is required. Croatian citizens abroad must register with a Croatian embassy or consulate to be entered in the military register — in the year they turn 18, or by 29 if not previously registered. This duty attaches to being a citizen, not to living in Croatia.
  • Call-up is not. Croatia has stated it will not send call-up notices to dual citizens residing abroad. Compulsory basic training (eight weeks, ages 19–29) applies to residents of Croatia.
  • Failing to register carries a fine of roughly €250–1,320.
  • Prior service elsewhere exempts you from basic training — this covers naturalised citizens too.
Still genuinely open

Whether US Selective Service registration counts as the "military training or civil service in another country" that grants exemption is not settled. Selective Service is registration for a potential draft, not service performed — so it most likely does not qualify. Worth one direct question to the consulate rather than an assumption.
